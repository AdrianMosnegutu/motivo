ABSTRACT

\vspace{0.5cm}
\hrule
\vspace{0.5cm}

Digital music production is traditionally dominated by visual, grid-based interfaces (Piano Rolls) that
provide precise control over individual notes but lack mechanisms for high-level structural abstraction.
Conversely, algorithmic music programming languages often prioritize real-time performance and live-coding,
introducing complex concurrency and timing management that can hinder offline, deterministic composition.

This thesis presents the design, implementation, and evaluation of a novel Domain-Specific Language (DSL)
tailored for structured, algorithmic music composition. The proposed language treats musical motifs as
modular, reusable functions (patterns) and provides imperative control flow (loops, conditionals) to
procedurally generate complex arrangements.

The core contribution of this work is a "zero-runtime" compiler architecture implemented in modern C++23. The
compiler performs rigorous semantic validation and type-checking before utilizing a stateful "Lowering
Engine" to recursively unroll the hierarchical Abstract Syntax Tree into a flat timeline of discrete musical
events. This Intermediate Representation is then serialized into a standard MIDI Format 1 binary file. By
decoupling musical logic from temporal resolution, the compiler guarantees deterministic, phase-aligned
polyphony without the latency or thread-synchronization issues inherent to interpreted systems.

To validate the language, a full-stack web playground was developed, integrating the C++ compiler via a
Node.js backend to provide real-time compilation diagnostics, syntax highlighting, and in-browser synthesis.
The evaluation demonstrates that the DSL significantly reduces the cognitive overhead and boilerplate
required to construct complex rhythmic and harmonic structures, achieving high compression ratios compared to
manual MIDI event placement. Ultimately, this thesis establishes a robust framework for treating musical
composition as a form of structured data, empowering composers to leverage the principles of software
engineering in their creative workflows.
