\section{Evaluation Summary}\label{sec:evaluation-summary}

The evaluation supports three bounded conclusions.
First, Motivo's compiler can expand compact source rules into much larger MIDI event streams, as shown by fixtures B1
(20,000 events from 8 lines), B2 (9,980 events from 8 lines), and B3 (20,000 events from 12 nested loops).
Second, version~1.0 and version~2.0 both compile these programs in about 5--8~ms end-to-end on a local Apple M3 Pro
Release build, with version~2.0 imposing roughly $0$--$2\%$ wall-clock overhead while producing identical MIDI output.
Third, the implemented system is backed by 745 automated tests (635 compiler, 31 server, 79 client) and CI across
compiler, server, client, and paper workflows.

These conclusions are bounded by the scope stated in Section~\ref{sec:evaluation-methodology} and
Section~\ref{sec:limits}.
