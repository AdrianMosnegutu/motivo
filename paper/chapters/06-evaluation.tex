\chapter{Empirical Evaluation}\label{ch:evaluation}

This chapter tests whether Motivo's structural compression works in practice: can compact symbolic source expand
deterministically into much larger MIDI timelines, fast enough for interactive use, without a playback-time runtime?
The evidence is drawn from a reproducible benchmark suite (Section~\ref{sec:evaluation-methodology}) and is organized
around the compiler's four pipeline stages.
Section~\ref{sec:compile-time} measures how compile time decomposes across those stages and grows with the size of the
generated score; Section~\ref{sec:scaling-stress} holds the output fixed and varies the shape of the source program;
Section~\ref{sec:general-case} turns to real compositions; and Section~\ref{sec:compression} quantifies the
source-to-event expansion that motivates the language.
Section~\ref{sec:limits} states the implementation limits, and Section~\ref{sec:comparative-interpretation} places the
results in the context of related tools.
No user study was conducted; musical quality and learnability are out of scope.
