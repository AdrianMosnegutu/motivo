\section{Closing Remarks}\label{sec:closing}

Motivo demonstrates that compiler design can be applied to a creative symbolic domain in a concrete and measurable way.
The language gives repeated musical ideas a compact textual form; the version~2.0 compiler validates that form with
explicit static typing and expands it into MIDI events through a staged pipeline; Motivo Studio places the result inside
a deployable product with editing, feedback, visualization, playback, automated tests, and production hosting.

The system is not a replacement for DAWs, live-coding environments, or synthesis platforms.
It is a focused bridge between algorithmic structure and Standard MIDI-based production workflows---strong where rules
and repetition dominate, weaker where hand-placed micro-editing, expressive MIDI, and real-time manipulation matter
most.
That bounded scope is both a limitation and a strength: it kept the language implementable, the compiler evaluable, and
the engineering story coherent within a bachelor's thesis.
