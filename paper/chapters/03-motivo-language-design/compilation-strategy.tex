\section{Compilation Strategy}\label{sec:compilation-strategy}

Motivo follows a \textit{zero-runtime} model: loops, branches, and pattern calls are expanded during compilation,
not interpreted at playback.
Lowering produces a flat intermediate representation of timestamped events, which the MIDI backend serializes.
Semantic errors are reported before lowering begins; strict typing and single-pass analysis keep that pipeline
predictable.
